Badshahi Masjid

The Badshahi Masjid is located in Lahore, built in 1673 by Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb. It is one of the largest mosques in the world accomodating over 55,000 worshippers, making it a symbol of spirtual and architechtural grandeur.

Faisal Mosque

The Faisal Mosque is located in Islamabad, built in 1986 by King Faisal bin Abdulaziz of Saudi Arabia, and designed by Turkish architect Vedat Dalokay. It features a unique and modern design inspired by a Bedouin tent. Accomodating over 100,000 worshippers, it serves as a symbol of Islamic unity and architectural innovation.

Hunza Valley

Hunza Valley is located in northern Pakistan; it is a stunning mountainous region known for its breathtaking landscapes, vibrant culture, and hospitable people. Surrounded by the Karakoram Range, it features snow-capped peaks, terraced fields, and historic forts like Baltit and Altit. A popular tourist destination, it offers trekking, cultural experiences, and serene beauty.

K2

K2 is located in Karakoram Range on Pakistan-China border. It is the second highest mountain in the world standing at 28,251ft. Due to its extreme difficulty and high fatality rate for climbers it is nicknamed "Savage Mountain". It stands as a symbol of challenge and adventure for mountineers all over the world.

Lahore Fort

The Lahore Fort is located in Lahore, originally built in the 11th century during Ghazanavi dynasty, but most of its present structure was developed during the Mughal period by Akbar, Shah Jehan, and Aurangzeb. Also known as "Shahi Qila" it showcases stunning Mughal architecture and impressive structures. Being a UNESCO World Heritage site, it holds cultural and histroical significance.

Mohatta Palace

The Mohatta Palace is located in Karachi, built in 1927 by a wealthy Hindu businessman Shiv Rattan Mohatta. It features a unique blend of Rajasthani and Islamic architectural styles. It was initially used for residence then later converted into a museum. It serves as a cultural center showcasing art, history, and the regions heritage.

Pakistan Monument

The Pakistan Monument is located in Islamabad, built in 2004 by a Pakistani architect Shahid Abrar. It serves as a national symbol representing the four provinces of Pakistan. Designed as the shape of a flower, each petal representing a province; Punjab, Sindh, Balochistan, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. It stands as a proud representation of the country's unity.

Swat Valley

Swat Valley is known as the 'Switzerland of the East' for its natural beauty. From river streams to lush valleys, it offers tranquility and adventure alike.

Wazir Khan Masjid

The Wazir Khan Masjid in Lahore is a vibrant 17th-century mosque known for its elaborate frescoes and tile work. It's an architectural gem of the Mughal era.
